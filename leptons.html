Particle Guide

What Are Leptons?

Leptons are fundamental particles in the Standard Model. They include the electron, muon, tau, and three neutrinos. Unlike quarks, leptons do not experience the strong force, which makes them a distinct family of matter particles.

The Six Leptons

The six leptons are the electron, muon, tau, electron neutrino, muon neutrino, and tau neutrino. The electron is especially important because it determines atomic structure and drives chemistry.

Why Neutrinos Matter

Neutrinos are extremely light and interact very weakly with matter, allowing them to pass through planets, stars, and people with barely any collisions. They are central to weak interaction physics and modern astrophysics.

Leptons vs Quarks

Quarks are confined inside hadrons and feel the strong force. Leptons do not feel the strong force and can exist as isolated particles, such as the electron. This difference is one of the clearest distinctions between the two matter families.
