Particle Guide

What Are Bosons?

Bosons are particles associated with forces and field interactions in the Standard Model. They include the photon, gluon, W boson, Z boson, and Higgs boson. Because bosons transmit interactions, they are often described as force carriers.

Force-Carrying Bosons

The photon carries electromagnetism. Gluons carry the strong force. The W and Z bosons carry the weak force. Together these explain how particles attract, bind, decay, and transform.

The Higgs Boson

The Higgs boson is different from the gauge bosons because it is linked to the Higgs field, the mechanism that gives many particles mass. Its discovery at CERN in 2012 was one of the most important confirmations in modern physics.
