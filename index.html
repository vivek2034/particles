Particle Families

Three essential groups in the quantum zoo

Each family plays a distinct role in how the universe stores mass, carries charge, and transmits forces.

Quarks

Quarks are elementary particles that come in six flavors: up, down, charm, strange, top, and bottom. They are never observed alone because the strong interaction confines them into composite particles such as protons, neutrons, and mesons.

Key idea: quarks build hadrons and help define the structure of atomic nuclei.

Read the quarks guide

Leptons

Leptons include the electron, muon, tau, and their corresponding neutrinos. Unlike quarks, leptons do not feel the strong force. Electrons are central to chemistry, while neutrinos are famous for passing through matter with very little interaction.

Key idea: leptons shape atomic behavior and reveal weak-interaction physics.

Read the leptons guide

Bosons

Bosons are force-carrying particles that mediate interactions. Photons transmit electromagnetism, gluons bind quarks through the strong force, and W and Z bosons govern the weak force. The Higgs boson is linked to the mechanism that gives many particles mass.

Key idea: bosons are the messengers that let particles influence one another.
