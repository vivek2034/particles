Particle Guide

What Are Quarks?

Quarks are elementary particles in the Standard Model of particle physics. They are the building blocks of hadrons, including protons and neutrons, which means quarks help form the atomic nuclei at the center of ordinary matter.

The Six Quark Flavors

Physicists classify quarks into six flavors: up, down, charm, strange, top, and bottom. The up and down quarks are the most common in everyday matter because protons and neutrons are built from them. The heavier quarks appear in high-energy conditions such as particle collisions.

Up quark

Charge: +2/3. Found in protons and neutrons.

Down quark

Charge: -1/3. Also central to ordinary matter.

Charm quark

Heavier quark seen in energetic interactions.

Strange quark

Important in strange hadrons and kaons.

Top quark

The heaviest known elementary particle.

Bottom quark

Appears in bottom hadrons and decay chains.

Quark Confinement and Color Charge

Quarks are never observed alone in normal conditions because of a property called confinement. They interact through the strong force, carried by gluons, and this force becomes very strong when quarks are pulled apart. This is why quarks remain bound inside hadrons. Physicists describe this interaction with a property called color charge, which is unrelated to visible color but essential in quantum chromodynamics.

How Quarks Form Protons and Neutrons

A proton is made of two up quarks and one down quark. A neutron is made of two down quarks and one up quark. These combinations explain the electric charges of the particles and show why quarks are essential to the structure of atoms and matter itself.
